# Human Glue LinkedIn Viral Content Hook Library

## Strategic Overview

This library contains 30+ viral-ready content hooks designed for Human Glue's LinkedIn launch. Each hook is engineered to stop the scroll, trigger emotional response, and generate shares among C-suite executives frustrated with stalled AI transformations.

**Core Thesis Embedded Throughout:** Most AI transformations fail because they ignore the reality gap between leadership perception and employee experience.

**Target Audience:** C-suite executives, CHROs, COOs, and transformation leaders at mid-to-large enterprises (500-5,000 employees)

## CATEGORY 1: CONFESSION/VULNERABILITY HOOKS

These hooks leverage authenticity and counter-signaling to build trust. Executives are tired of consultants who never admit fault.

**Opening Lines:**

- "I was wrong" format is rare from consultants
- Promise of revelation ("what we got wrong")

**Emotional Response:**
- Relief (someone else made this mistake)
- Curiosity (what was the actual answer?)
- Trust (vulnerability breeds connection)

**Shareability Score:** 9/10
*Executives will screenshot this to share with their L&D teams as validation for their concerns about training-only approaches.*

## POSTING BEST PRACTICES

### Optimal Post Structure:
1. Hook (2 lines max)
2. Line break
3. Story/explanation (3-5 short paragraphs)
4. Key insight or framework
5. Question or call to action

### Engagement Optimization:
- Post Tuesday-Thursday, 7-8am or 5-6pm in target timezone
- Respond to every comment in first 2 hours
- Tag relevant connections who would find value
- Use no more than 3-5 hashtags
- End with a question to drive comments

### Visual Recommendations:
- Create carousel versions for data-heavy hooks
- Use simple charts for statistical contrasts
- Consider "quote graphic" format for strongest single-line hooks

### Content Calendar Suggestion:
- Week 1-2: Confession hooks (build trust)
- Week 3-4: Data bomb hooks (establish expertise)
- Week 5-6: Named enemy hooks (create shared language)
- Week 7-8: Contrarian hooks (build thought leadership)
- Ongoing: Mix all categories with question hooks for engagement
